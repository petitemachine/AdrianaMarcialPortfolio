UX STUDY CASE

MAYNOOTH FURNITURE

01

PROJECT

The company redefined their target audience, finding their Persona in a 60-year-old woman keen on interior design. Working mostly on my own, I created a poll to gather feedback from people similar to my persona. They loved the colours and style — exciting to see their desire to see more from Maynooth Furniture, even as a prototype.

02

OBJECTIVE

The company gave me complete creative freedom. My priority was designing for someone who might wear glasses: bigger text, classic colours, large buttons. At every step I thought like my persona, considering her possible limitations. The intended outcome was a lovely, easy-to-navigate website experience.

03

RESULT

Insights were translated into design recommendations. Collaboration shaped strategy and product decisions. Research influenced UX choices around accessibility, colour palette, and navigation — all validated through user testing with participants closely matching the persona profile.

04

WORK

I worked in constant communication with the company. Participants were ladies of similar age — nearly retired with a lifestyle matching the user persona. I rearranged the poll into 3 stages: introducing the company, a follow-up questionnaire, and finally completing the task — all without instructions provided.

05

REFLECTION

After completing the project, we were very satisfied with the responses and conversations with people matching our Persona. The biggest lesson: listen attentively and take every comment into consideration — using data to make changes and decisions effectively.
